# Product Vision

## Product Name

Astra: Spatial Intelligence for Large Public Spaces

## Executive Summary

Large public gatherings generate enormous operational complexity. Kumbh Mela, religious festivals, rail stations, airports, stadiums, disaster relief camps, and smart-city districts all depend on fast decisions about crowd movement, infrastructure capacity, public safety, sanitation, medical response, and emergency access.

Most existing surveillance systems answer only one question:

```text
What is happening right now?
```

Astra is designed to answer more useful operational questions:

- What will happen next?
- Where will congestion form?
- Which routes are becoming unsafe?
- Which facilities will exceed capacity?
- What intervention should authorities take?
- What is the expected impact of that intervention?

Astra transforms visual and spatial observations into operational intelligence. It builds a continuously updated Spatial Knowledge Graph and uses it to forecast risk, recommend action, and preserve an auditable operational timeline.

## Core Insight

The strongest idea is not crowd counting. The strongest idea is crowd flow intelligence.

Counting people at one camera is descriptive. Estimating how people move through a network is operational. Authorities can act on predicted flow, congestion risk, and route capacity before unsafe bottlenecks form.

## Vision

Astra is an AI-powered digital twin and decision-support layer for public operations. It models the physical environment as a living graph of places, paths, zones, sensors, risks, resources, events, predictions, and actions.

The long-term platform should support:

- Crowd intelligence
- Emergency routing
- Medical response
- Sanitation and water demand forecasting
- Parking and transport flow
- Drowning, flood, fire, heat, and structural risk
- Lost and found workflows
- Volunteer and police deployment
- Post-event replay and planning

## Guiding Principle

```text
Cameras should not merely record events.
Cities should understand themselves.
```

## Platform Lifecycle

Every capability follows the same loop:

```text
Observe -> Understand -> Predict -> Recommend -> Act -> Learn
```

## Users

Primary users:

- Event command center operators
- Police and crowd-control teams
- Disaster management authorities
- Medical and ambulance coordinators
- Municipal operations teams

Secondary users:

- Volunteers
- Sanitation teams
- Transport coordinators
- Planning departments
- Citizens receiving route or safety guidance

## Differentiation

Astra is not positioned as a camera analytics tool. Camera analytics is an input layer.

Astra is differentiated by:

- A domain-specific Spatial Knowledge Graph
- Semantic modeling of civic infrastructure
- Flow and risk prediction across a network
- Recommendation generation, not just dashboards
- Replayable event history for audit and learning
- A common ontology that can expand beyond crowd management

## Long-Term Direction

The hackathon proof of concept validates one thin slice: crowd flow prediction at Kumbh Mela.

The broader product direction is a civic operating layer that can be adapted to temporary cities, permanent city districts, transport networks, and large campuses by changing the ontology and graph data, not rewriting the intelligence engine.
